Travel Costs

As an IEEE Young Professionals leader, there are potentially several sources of funding available to subsidize or cover your travel, lodging, and registration.

  • The $300 2026 IEEE Regions 1 and 2 Young Professionals Travel Grant. Contact Sam Stone, IEEE Region 1 YP Chair, at stone@ieee.org or Alexis Gorgacz, IEEE Region 2 YP Chair, at agorgacz@ieee.org for more information.
  • Your IEEE Section. Talk to your Section Chair and attend your Section ExCom to inquire.
  • Other IEEE Organizational Units, such as your Chapter or Society.
  • Your employer.

How to Get to Boston

Boston is easily accessible from across IEEE Regions 1 and 2:

  • Direct flights to Boston Logan International Airport (BOS)
  • By Amtrak, MBTA Commuter Rail, and regional bus services
  • By Car
Regardless of how you get to Boston, we recommend using the MBTA subway system (the "T") and commuter rail to get around. The subway connects downtown Boston with major neighborhoods, universities, and transportation hubs. Logan Airport is also connected to the city via the Silver Line and airport shuttle services. If you are coming by car, consider parking at your hotel or using park-and-ride garages near MBTA stations, as parking in downtown Boston can be limited and expensive.
